Somnambulists

A somnambulist cannot be held to account for actions carried out while sleepwalking. This is because they are typically unaware of what they've done, what they've eaten or where they've gone while sleepwalking.

Treatments for Somnambulists

  1. Therapy: a few research have shown the merits of therapy. Albeit anecdotal, it is worthy to note that therapy and hypnosis have been shown to have effects in weaning some sufferers off certain maladies.
  2. Palliative Treatment: this include close monitoring of the somnambulist when he or she sleeps to catch the instances where an episode starts to see if there is a notable precursor to an episode.
  3. Talk Therapy: this involves speaking with the somnabulists to make them realise and accept the realities of the condition. Encourage them to be comfprtable with it and to freely share its realities woth a significant other.
